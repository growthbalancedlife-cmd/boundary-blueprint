How to use this workbook

This page is a living practice space for the prompts and exercises in The Boundary Blueprint guide. Move through each step at your own pace. Treat what you write here as data, not judgment.

You can revisit these exercises as your relationships, responsibilities, and capacity change over time.

Step 1 · Awareness

Map one week of quiet “yes” and “no” decisions

Before changing any boundaries, simply notice them. For the next 7 days, track where you say “yes” to others and quietly say “no” to yourself.

Daily log

Each day, write down 3–5 moments where you said “yes”. Then ask:

  • What did I say yes to?
  • What did I quietly say no to (time, energy, rest, creativity)?
  • How did my body feel right after?

Pattern snapshot

At the end of the week, look for patterns:

  • Where do my “yes” decisions cluster (work, family, community)?
  • What do I most often sacrifice?
  • What would one honest boundary look like in this pattern?

Use this week as data, not judgment. You’re learning how your current boundaries actually move in real life before you try to redraw them.

Step 2 · Naming

Draft one honest boundary statement

Based on the patterns you noticed, write one boundary statement that protects your time, energy, or emotional capacity in a specific situation.

Situation

Describe the situation where you want to set a boundary.

Boundary statement

Draft a boundary using simple, honest language.

Choose one small context to test this boundary and notice how your body and relationships respond. You can refine the language over time.
